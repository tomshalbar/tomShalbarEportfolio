Reflection- First year at UF

Reflecting on my first year at the University of Florida, I'm grateful for how much I've grown both academically and personally. As a computer science major, I was challenged in ways that pushed me to develop stronger problem-solving and time management skills. Courses introduced me to the foundations of programming and algorithms, setting a solid base for the future. Beyond the classroom, being involved in the SEC (Software Engineering Club) and OS (Open source) Club gave me a chance to connect with peers who share my interests. Through projects, workshops, and guest talks, I gained hands-on experience and insight into industry practices that go beyond textbook learning. These communities helped me stay motivated and excited about the field. Looking back, I'm proud of what I've accomplished and even more excited for what's ahead. My first year at UF has set a strong foundation for both my education and career.

Reflection- Second year at UF

During this year, I reached a couple major milestones in my academic career. For starters, I started my first computer science job. At the Applied Artificial Intelligence Group, I worked on major research projects, including a novel indexing system and a multimodal RAG engine. I learned a lot about proper software engineering practices, team work, the research process, and expanded my knowledge of building and training machine learning based systems. Another milestone I hit this year is getting an internship. Joining Blockaid during the summer of 2026 as a data science intern, I am looking forward to expanding my industry experience, learning about the cybersecurity world, and getting an insight into the way startups function.
